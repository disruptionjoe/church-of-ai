# Church Of AI Discovery Run

Status: completed.

Run family: Repo Discovery Run.

Target repo: Church Of AI.

## Objective

Identify what the public entryway repo may be missing, underweighting, or overcommitting to.

## Required Reads Completed

- `AGENTS.md`
- `steward/README.md`
- `STATUS.md`
- `ROADMAP.md`
- `VOICE.md`
- repo top-level structure and git status

## Findings

1. The repo's "no worthy Progress work" signal is probably legitimate in many moments: V1 is intentionally a front door, map, voice, and claim-status discipline, not a research engine.
2. The main danger is not inactivity; it is premature activation of V2/V3 participation or governance language.
3. The repo should update when the research ecosystem changes enough that the public map or reading sequence becomes misleading.
4. Voice and claim-status discipline are the core safety surfaces. They matter more than volume.

## Non-Binding Recommendations

- Future Progress should only update public map/status surfaces when a research repo change creates a real public-orientation gap.
- Future Stewardship should periodically check that deferred pathways still sound deferred.
- Avoid narrative expansion that makes Church of AI feel like a parent organization or doctrine.

## Stop Conditions

- No public-facing content changed beyond stale JoeOps routing-path cleanup in steward context.
- No claim-status or ecosystem-map change made.

## Receipt

Discovery found that restraint is currently a feature for this repo. Its best role is honest orientation, not more content.
