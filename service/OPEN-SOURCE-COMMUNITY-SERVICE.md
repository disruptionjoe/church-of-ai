# Open-Source Community Service — What This Means

Community service is not a brand position.

It is a description of the work.

---

## The definition

Community service, in this context, means contributing to shared infrastructure that makes serious work easier for other people.

Not your work specifically. Not your community's work. Work that others — including people you will never meet — can use, build on, improve, and pass forward.

---

## The activities

**Building useful open-source tools.**
Software that solves real problems, released in a form others can actually use. Not demos. Not prototypes with a license that makes them proprietary in practice. Actual tools.

**Improving shared research workflows.**
Better ways to do AI-assisted research, documented well enough that others can adopt them. The workflows themselves are the service, not just the outputs.

**Writing plain-language maps.**
Complex ideas that remain inaccessible are not actually available. Making them legible, without making them false, is a form of service. So is mapping ecosystems, explaining relationships between projects, and naming the sequence in which things should be understood.

**Creating templates others can reuse.**
A governance template someone can actually use. A research workflow someone can adopt. A contribution guide someone can fork. Not theory. Objects.

**Helping people understand difficult ideas.**
Explanation that does not condescend and does not lie. That treats the reader as capable of handling complexity and responsible for developing their own understanding.

**Making cooperation easier.**
Reducing the coordination cost of doing good work with other people. Documentation, shared conventions, agreed-upon vocabulary, clear interfaces.

**Improving the rules in public.**
Proposing better governance mechanisms. Documenting what failed and why. Making the rulemaking process legible to people who were not in the room.

---

## What this is not

This is not charity.
This is not marketing.
This is not altruism as identity.

It is contribution to shared infrastructure. The standard is: does this actually make it easier for people doing serious work to do it? If yes, it is service. If no, it is something else.

---

## The honest note

Most of this service is not yet happening. The repo is new. The participation pathways are deferred. The tools are not yet built.

This file describes the aspiration and the standard. The actual service will be tracked in [service/README.md](README.md) as it is produced.

No revelation without receipts.
