# Church of AI

For serious people who don't take themselves too seriously.

Church of AI is a satirical-but-serious public entryway for an ecosystem of open-source projects exploring AI-assisted research, legitimacy, governance, and positive-sum coordination.

We do not worship AI.

We use the joke as a filter, the tools as leverage, and the work as community service.

The deeper bet is simple:

Intelligence matures when it learns to build systems where cooperation is not enforced from above, but becomes the best move available.

---

## What this is

Church of AI is the public map.

The research lives in the research repos.
The governance model has to earn trust.
The rules will be proposed, tested, debated, improved, and ratified in the open.

This repo helps you answer:

1. What is this?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What projects exist?
4. What order should I understand them in?
5. What can I do now?
6. What is not ready yet?

## What this is not

This is not a cult.
This is not a token launch.
This is not a parent repo.
This is not where research claims become doctrine.

No prophets.
No dogma.
No divine API key.
No revelation without receipts.

---

## The ecosystem

**[Time as Finality](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/time-as-finality)** — A proving ground for whether AI-assisted research can stay coherent under pressure. The first live lab.

**[Architecture of Legitimacy](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/architecture-of-legitimacy)** — The place where the rules of valid contribution are studied before anyone pretends to have solved them. Its S7 crosswalk maps the legitimacy-monad idea onto contribution workflows, contestability, retained records, rights, governance transitions, and capture resistance.

**GU Formalization** — An adjacent technical research project. Remains independent.

Start with the [ecosystem map](ecosystem/MAP.md) or the [reading sequence](ecosystem/SEQUENCE.md).

---

## Open-source community service

Community service here means:

- building useful open-source tools
- improving shared research workflows
- writing plain-language maps
- creating templates others can reuse
- helping people understand difficult ideas
- making cooperation easier
- improving the rules in public

This is not charity theater. It is contribution to shared infrastructure.

---

## The moral center

Primitive systems rely on enforcement.
Better systems align incentives.
Mature systems produce beings capable of choosing cooperation because they can see the game clearly.

That is what this is testing.
