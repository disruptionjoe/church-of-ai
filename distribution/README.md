updates, and repo sequence without opening contribution, governance,
membership, rewards, or proving-ground participation.

## Candidate Channel Fit

- Substack: primary owned-audience home for plain-language maps and dated
  updates.
- Instagram Reels: easiest short-video starting point.
- YouTube Shorts: near-free mirror for evergreen, searchable clips.
- TikTok: optional mirror with higher policy volatility and weaker audience fit.
- X: out.

The efficient video pattern is one vertical clip that can be reused across the
chosen short-video channels.

These are candidate fits for future production planning. They are not a live
publishing plan, channel launch, or request for Joe to choose channels before
the production relationship is settled.

## Content Rules

- Use public Church and linked-repo content only.
- Preserve claim status: proven so far, being tested, hypothesized, or
  deferred.
- Allowed calls to action: read the map, follow the work, or read the update.
- Not allowed yet: come contribute, join, govern, claim membership, rewards,
  DAO/token framing, or proving-ground participation.
- Church explains and invites; it does not certify research truth or decide for
  other repositories.

## Setup Boundary

Agents may plan, draft repo-local content, and prepare mailbox proposals. Agents
may not create accounts, post, schedule, send, publish outside GitHub, spend, or
change external services. Those actions require Joe's explicit scoped approval,
and Joe performs them himself.
