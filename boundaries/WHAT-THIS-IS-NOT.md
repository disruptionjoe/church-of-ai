# What This Is Not

This page exists because the things this is not are easy to read into.

---

## Not a cult

The aesthetic is satirical. The name is a joke. The structure is open-source and anti-dogmatic.

If it starts to feel like a cult — unfalsifiable claims, founder mythology, pressure to agree, suspension of critical thinking — that is a failure mode, not the vision.

The joke filters the unserious. It is not an invitation to uncritical belief.

---

## Not a joke with no work behind it

The satirical wrapper has to be earned by the work inside it. If the humor is all there is, it is satire without content.

The work is in the research repos. The jokes are at the door.

---

## Not a token project

No token.
No DAO.
No presale.
No "community" that is actually a customer base for a financial instrument.

If this ever moves in that direction without a compelling, publicly documented, extensively tested reason, something has gone wrong.

---

## Not a research authority

Church of AI does not ratify research claims. It does not adjudicate between competing findings. It does not certify that any claim made in any ecosystem repo is correct.

The research repos are independent. They stand or fall on their own merits.

---

## Not a parent repo

No repo in this ecosystem is governed by Church of AI. No repo reports to Church of AI. No repo requires Church of AI's approval.

The relationship is narrative and coordinative, not hierarchical.

---

## Not a place where technical claims become doctrine

When a research project produces a finding, that finding does not automatically become a principle of the Church.

Claim-status discipline applies to everything. See [CLAIM-STATUS.md](CLAIM-STATUS.md).

---

## Not a place to pretend decentralization has been earned

Early governance is founder-led. That is honest. Decentralization is something the system has to earn through demonstrated trustworthiness over time.

Calling something decentralized before it is earns nothing and costs trust.

---

## Not a place for founder mythology

The founder is not a prophet. The origin story is not sacred. The early decisions were made by a person with limited information under real constraints.

Naming that is not self-deprecation. It is accuracy.
