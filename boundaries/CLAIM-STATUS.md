# Claim Status

Every major claim in this ecosystem should be treated as one of four things.

This is not bureaucracy. It is honesty about where we actually are.

---

## The four statuses

### Proven so far

Actually demonstrated in repo practice. Not just asserted. Demonstrated.

This category is small. That is correct. Claims move into this category slowly, with evidence.

### Being tested

Currently under active experiment. We have a hypothesis and we are running it.

This is where most active work lives. Being tested does not mean "probably true." It means we are in the process of finding out.

### Hypothesized

Plausible, but not yet tested. There is a reason to think this is true. The reason has not been put to a test that could falsify it.

A lot of interesting ideas live here. That is fine. Interesting ideas should be held carefully until they earn a different status.

### Deferred

Not ready to launch, govern, reward, or invite participation around.

Deferred is not "probably false." It is "the conditions are not right to test this yet." Often because the prerequisites are not in place.

---

## How to use this

When reading anything in this ecosystem, ask: what is the claim-status of this?

If the document doesn't say, assume hypothesized at best.

If someone is treating a hypothesized claim as proven, that is the beginning of dogma. Name it.

**"AI-assisted research can stay coherent under pressure."**

Status: Hypothesized, now testable. Architecture of Legitimacy has a [crosswalk](https://github.com/disruptionjoe/architecture-of-legitimacy/blob/main/explorations/legitimacy-monad-s7-crosswalk-2026-06-25.md) and proposed workflow test; it has not yet run the contribution-log experiment.

## The rule

No revelation without receipts.

Claims are labeled. Evidence is named. The distance between "seems promising" and "proven" is respected.
