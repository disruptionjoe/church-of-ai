# Principles

This folder collects the durable principles behind Church of AI.

They are not proof that the ecosystem has succeeded. They are not doctrine.
They are not participation rules or operational governance. They are orienting
commitments to test against practice while the project remains in its public
entryway phase.

## Start Here

- [Positive-sum coordination](POSITIVE-SUM.md) - the core wager that better
  systems can make cooperation the best available move.
- [No dogma](NO-DOGMA.md) - the discipline that keeps claims question-ready
  instead of identity-protected.
- [Open rules](OPEN-RULES.md) - the path toward rules that are visible,
  testable, revisable, and explained in the open.

## How To Read These

- Treat each principle as a standard to test against practice, not as evidence
  that the standard has already been met.
- Use [claim status](../boundaries/CLAIM-STATUS.md) when a principle becomes a
  claim about what has been demonstrated.
- Use [current status](../STATUS.md), [roadmap](../ROADMAP.md), and
  [participation boundaries](../participation/NOT-YET.md) to check what is live
  now and what remains deferred.

If a principle starts sounding sacred, route back to [No dogma](NO-DOGMA.md)
and ask for receipts.
