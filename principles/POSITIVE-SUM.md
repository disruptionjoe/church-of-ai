# Positive-Sum Coordination

The highest form of intelligence is not domination.

It is the ability to recognize and build positive-sum games.

---

## What positive-sum means here

A positive-sum game is one where participants can both win. Not equally, not without tradeoffs, but without requiring the other's loss as the condition for your success.

Most of the coordination failures worth worrying about are not caused by bad people. They are caused by systems where the optimal individual move is destructive to the whole - where defecting beats cooperating, where cheating beats contributing, where extraction beats investment.

The research question this ecosystem keeps returning to is: what would it take to build systems where that is not true?

Not through enforcement. Not through assuming people are better than they are. Through design that makes cooperation the path of least resistance.

---

## Why this matters for AI

AI systems are new pressure on old coordination problems.

They make defection faster. They make extraction more efficient. They make the detection of bad actors harder. And they make the gap between what is technically possible and what is socially agreed-upon wider than it has ever been.

The response to this is not panic. It is the same thing the response to every coordination problem has been: build better systems.

Positive-sum coordination is not a utopian goal. It is an engineering problem.

---

## What this principle requires in practice

- Do not design for a world of purely rational actors or purely good-faith actors. Design for real humans with real incentives.
- When a rule is proposed, ask: who does this help and who does it hurt, and is that distribution defensible?
- Prefer systems where bad actors are naturally excluded by the structure, not by trust.
- When a contribution benefits the contributor more than the whole, say so. That is not always wrong. It is always worth naming.
- Keep asking: is this actually positive-sum, or does it just feel good?

---

## What this principle does not mean

- It does not mean everyone gets the same outcome.
- It does not mean conflict is bad.
- It does not mean all cooperation is good.
- It does not mean the collective always beats the individual.

Positive-sum is a property of specific interactions in specific contexts. It is not a universal claim.

The current evidence label for positive-sum coordination claims lives in
