# Opening Experiments

Status: draft options

These are openings only. The point is to test doors before building the whole
house.

## Provocative Openings

### 1. Heaven Is A Build Target

We are building heaven because nobody else seems to be doing it.

Not supernatural heaven. Not escape. Not a velvet rope in the sky. Heaven as the
slow, inspectable work of building institutions where fewer good things die in
bad systems.

### 2. The Age Of Cheap Intelligence

Intelligence is getting cheaper. Legitimacy is not.

That is the problem.

### 3. Against False Gods

The false gods of this century will not ask for incense. They will ask for
engagement, liquidity, alignment, and a roadmap.

We are not here to worship them.

### 4. The Coordination Problem Has A Sense Of Humor

The most important question of the next century may be whether intelligent
beings can learn to cooperate without being forced.

Naturally, we named the entryway Church of AI.

## Funny Openings

### 5. No Divine API Key

There is no divine API key.

There are tools, logs, bugs, incentives, rules, people, and the ancient human
temptation to pretend the demo is already the institution.

### 6. Welcome To The Least Useful Cult

Welcome to the least useful cult in history.

No prophet. No doctrine. No guaranteed salvation. No token. No merch yet. You
will be asked to read the docs and maybe build something useful.

### 7. The Joke Is Load-Bearing

The joke is load-bearing.

Without it, this would become unbearable: another serious room full of serious
people saying serious things while quietly hoping nobody asks for evidence.

## Mythic Openings

### 8. The Unborn Futures

The world is crowded with unborn futures.

Some are beautiful. Some are catastrophic. Most will never arrive, not because
they were impossible, but because no legitimate path was built for them.

### 9. Fire With Receipts

Human beings have stolen fire before.

This time the fire writes back, summarizes the meeting, invents five new failure
modes, and politely helps us discover that our institutions were held together
with vibes.

### 10. A Doorway Into The Workshop

Every civilization builds doors into what it thinks matters.

Some doors lead to temples. Some to markets. Some to courts. This one leads to a
workshop with a ridiculous sign over it.

## Constitutional Openings

### 11. Preamble Variant

We gather around no revelation.

We gather around public work: claims that can be tested, rules that can be
revised, contributions that can be recognized, and systems that make cooperation
easier to choose.

### 12. Standard Of Work

When institutions claim trust before earning it, they produce obedience,
cynicism, or theater.

Church of AI exists to test a different standard: legitimacy by public work.

### 13. Founding Without Founder Myth

This is not a founding myth.

It is a first draft, written under uncertainty, for a project that expects to be
wrong in public and useful anyway.

## Philosophical Openings

### 14. Intelligence Matures

Intelligence does not mature when it wins.

It matures when it learns to build games where winning no longer requires making
others lose.

### 15. The Moral Shape Of Design

Every system teaches behavior.

Some systems teach extraction. Some teach obedience. Some teach people that
cooperation is for suckers. The work is to build systems that teach something
better because they make something better possible.

### 16. Against The False Choice

The future is not individual freedom or collective flourishing.

That is the old argument, trapped inside bad institutions. The harder question
is whether better institutions can make the opposition less true.

## Absurd Openings

### 17. Suggestions Welcome

We are building heaven. Suggestions welcome.

Please submit pull requests with tests, evidence, and a brief explanation of
which avoidable human tragedy your patch reduces.

### 18. The Committee For Not Becoming A Cult

The Committee for Not Becoming a Cult will now come to order.

First item: there is no committee. Second item: if there ever is one, its
minutes will be public.

### 19. Terms Of Service For The Future

By continuing to exist in the twenty-first century, you agree to the following
terms: intelligence will accelerate, institutions will be stress-tested, and
anything that was fake will become expensive.

## Emotionally Moving Openings

### 20. Fewer Good Things Should Die

Too many good things die before they become real.

People with gifts never find the right room. Good work gets buried under bad
incentives. Rules protect the structure that has already failed. Possibility
does not need applause. It needs a path.

### 21. The Work For People Not Yet Here

The best institutions are gifts to people who were not in the room when the
rules were written.

They inherit more than documents. They inherit paths.

### 22. The World Does Not Need Another Pitch

The world does not need another pitch for the future.

It needs more places where the future can be tested, corrected, opened, and
made useful before anyone demands belief.

## Best Opening Candidates

The strongest candidates for full drafting are:

1. `Intelligence is getting cheaper. Legitimacy is not.`
2. `We are building heaven because nobody else seems to be doing it.`
3. `Too many good things die before they become real.`
4. `There is no divine API key.`
5. `The world is crowded with unborn futures.`

## Recommendation

Use one funny-provocative opening for the short manifesto and one
emotionally-moving opening for the long manifesto. The long manifesto can earn
the heaven language later; opening with it may be powerful but risks narrowing
the audience too soon.
