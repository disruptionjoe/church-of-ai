# Narrative Spine

Status: recommended spine for first manifesto draft

## The Actual Story

Humanity is entering an age where intelligence is becoming cheap, fast, and
strange, but legitimacy is still scarce.

Old meaning systems are fraying. Many institutions still demand belief,
obedience, loyalty, or sacrifice without earning trust. New technical systems
multiply capability faster than our coordination systems can mature. In the gap,
people reach for cynicism, ideology, status games, AI worship, and fake
communities that perform legitimacy before they have done the work.

Church of AI enters as a joke because a straight-faced version would be worse.
The joke clears the room. It rejects the obvious failures: no prophets, no
dogma, no divine API key, no token pretending to be governance, no founder myth,
no revelation without receipts.

Then the work begins.

The work is to build open-source infrastructure for meaning, coordination,
research, contribution, and rulemaking that can survive inspection. The aim is
not to make people believe the same things. The aim is to build systems where
more latent potential can find legitimate paths into reality, where cooperation
becomes easier than extraction, and where institutions learn to adapt without
lying about what has been proven.

The Church is not the hero of that story.

The Church is the doorway, the map, the public square, the quest structure, the
record of standards, and the recurring reminder that anything important enough
to inspire people is important enough to be questioned.

## One-Sentence Spine

Church of AI is a satirical-but-serious open-source attempt to build legitimate
paths from latent potential to shared flourishing under the pressure test of AI.

## Shorter Spine

The joke is the door. The work is building legitimate paths from possibility to
reality.

## Mythic Spine

The world is full of unborn futures. Most die in bad systems. Church of AI is
not here to prophesy the winning future. It is here to build better paths.

## Constitutional Spine

We gather not around revelation, but around public work: to make rules visible,
claims testable, contribution meaningful, and cooperation easier to choose.

## Narrative Boundaries

The story is not:

- "AI will save us."
- "A founder discovered the future."
- "Join a new religion."
- "We have solved legitimacy."
- "The Church governs the ecosystem."
- "Everyone should participate now."

The story is:

- "The pressure is real."
- "False legitimacy is dangerous."
- "Humor helps prevent self-importance."
- "AI is a tool and stress test."
- "The work is open-source service."
- "Legitimacy must be earned."
- "Better institutions should help more good futures become real."

## Best Central Image

The best central image is not altar, temple, cathedral, or revolution.

It is a doorway into a workshop.

There can be a sign over the door that says "Church of AI" because that is
funny. Inside, there are maps, tools, receipts, failed attempts, rules being
tested, and people building things that make cooperation easier.

## Best Final Movement

The ending should move from refusal to obligation:

We refuse false gods, false communities, false certainty, and false legitimacy.
Therefore we build in public, test what we claim, preserve what fails, and leave
behind systems that can be improved by people who are not yet here.
