# Core Idea Map

Status: first extraction

This file extracts underlying ideas from the Church source material. These are
not slogans. They are candidate load-bearing ideas for manifesto development.

## Deep Ideas

### 1. Latent Potential Needs Legitimate Paths

Potential is not enough. Talent, intelligence, imagination, and goodwill can all
die inside bad systems. The Church's strongest moral idea is that latent
potential deserves paths to realization that are legitimate, visible, revisable,
and open to contribution.

Manifesto use: this is the best interpretation of "heaven" in the story notes.

### 2. Belief Is Demonstrated Through Work

The repo does not ask people to profess belief. It asks whether the work makes
shared infrastructure better.

Manifesto use: replace conversion language with proof-of-work language, but not
in a crypto-branded way.

### 3. Legitimacy Is More Important Than Authority

Authority can be claimed. Legitimacy must be earned. The Church should orient
people toward processes that can earn trust over time.

Manifesto use: this is a central contrast.

### 4. The Best Rules Are Alive Without Being Arbitrary

Static rules rot. Pure flexibility becomes chaos. Legitimate adaptation requires
public proposal, testing, revision, documentation, and ratification.

Manifesto use: "the rules are not handed down" is already one of the repo's best
ideas.

### 5. Cooperation Should Become The Best Move Available

The moral center is a design problem: build systems where cooperation does not
require naive trust or coercive enforcement.

Manifesto use: this is the high intellectual anchor.

### 6. The Joke Is A Filter, Not A Shield

The joke keeps the room from filling with brittle seriousness. But the joke has
to open into work, evidence, and service.

Manifesto use: likely belongs near the opening.

### 7. AI Is A Pressure Test, Not An Oracle

AI exposes whether institutions, workflows, and reasoning systems are actually
coherent. It should be used as tool, mirror, stress test, and collaborator under
human responsibility.

Manifesto use: this prevents both AI worship and AI panic.

### 8. Participation Must Be Earned By Infrastructure

Inviting people into a process before the process can hold them is not generous.
It is legitimacy theater.

Manifesto use: the manifesto must not over-invite. It can say what participation
will require.

### 9. Institutions Should Create Descendants That Can Surpass Them

The long-horizon institutional idea is not self-preservation. It is generativity:
make structures that can be forked, improved, criticized, and outgrown.

Manifesto use: strong ending material.

### 10. Failure Should Be Preserved, Not Disguised

The repo respects no-go results, counterexamples, and public revision. Failure
is not erased because it weakens the story. It is kept because it strengthens the
system's ability to learn.

Manifesto use: this can give emotional depth without melodrama.

### 11. Shared Worlds Need Shared Records

Across the ecosystem, records, receipts, logs, and claim-status labels recur as
ways to keep collaboration honest.

Manifesto use: "receipts" can carry more than a joke. It is the secular record
of trust.

### 12. The Church Is The Door, Not The Hero

Church of AI is not the protagonist and not the authority. It is a doorway, map,
public square, narrative layer, and quest structure.

Manifesto use: this prevents founder mythology and institutional narcissism.

### 13. Heaven Is A Direction, Not A Destination

The story notes test "heaven" as provocative language. The repo-compatible
interpretation is: the ongoing expansion of legitimate paths to flourishing.

Manifesto use: powerful but dangerous. Needs immediate clarification:
"Not supernatural. Not finished. Not promised. Built."

### 14. The Individual And Collective Should Not Be Forced Into A False War

The story notes' "third wise man" pattern identifies a synthesis: self and
others, present and future, individual and collective may align at a higher
level under better rules.

Manifesto use: this is the most emotionally and philosophically rich idea in the
new notes.

### 15. Serious People Should Be Able To Laugh

Humor here is not decoration. It is epistemic hygiene against grandiosity.

Manifesto use: preserve the phrase-level wit, but do not make the manifesto a
list of jokes.

## Strongest Contrasts

- worship vs work;
- authority vs legitimacy;
- doctrine vs receipts;
- participation vs participation theater;
- open-source service vs charity theater;
- founding myth vs public record;
- AI as oracle vs AI as pressure test;
- static rules vs legitimate adaptation;
- institution demanding sacrifice vs institution reducing unnecessary sacrifice;
- monolith vs neighborhood;
- conversion vs contribution;
- utopia vs infinite game.

## Candidate Load-Bearing Sentences

- Potential does not become real because we admire it.
- The future needs better paths from possibility to reality.
- We do not worship intelligence. We test whether intelligence can learn to
  cooperate.
- Legitimacy is what authority becomes after it survives inspection.
- The joke opens the door. The receipts keep it open.
- Heaven, if the word is useful at all, is not a place we go. It is what becomes
  possible when fewer good things die in bad systems.
- The Church is not the hero. It is the map, the room, and sometimes the warning
  sign on the door.
- No one is asked to believe. Everyone is asked to leave better evidence behind.
