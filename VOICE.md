# VOICE.md - Church of AI

Voice, claim-status discipline, and "what not to do" rules for anyone (human or agent)
writing content in this repo. (Renamed from AGENT.md 2026-06-30; the steward operating
contract lives in `AGENTS.md`.)

## Voice

This repo has a specific voice. Before writing anything:

- Serious, but never pompous
- Funny, but not unserious
- Open-source, not occult
- Principled, not preachy
- Ambitious, but allergic to grandiosity
- Transparent about what is proven, experimental, speculative, and deferred

Use ASCII punctuation in this repo. Prefer `-`, `->`, straight quotes, and
plain apostrophes over typographic dashes, arrows, or smart quotes. This keeps
the public docs stable across plain terminals, Windows shells, and API logs.

Core phrases that should feel natural here:

- "For serious people who don't take themselves too seriously."
- "No prophets. No dogma. No divine API key."
- "No revelation without receipts."
- "The joke is the doorway. The work is the point."

## What this repo is

A public map and narrative entryway. Not a research repo, not a parent org, not a governance authority.

Write accordingly. Content here explains and invites. It does not claim, govern, or decide.

## Claim-status discipline

Every major claim in this repo must be one of four things:

- **Proven so far** - actually demonstrated in repo practice
- **Being tested** - currently under active experiment
- **Hypothesized** - plausible, but not yet earned
- **Deferred** - not ready to launch, govern, or invite public participation around

When in doubt, use `CLAIM-STATUS.md` as the reference.

## What not to do

- Do not write founder mythology
- Do not absorb GU Formalization into the Church narrative
- Do not pretend decentralization has been earned
- Do not make participation pathways sound live when they are deferred
- Do not let technical claims become doctrine
- Do not add sections that sound like a DAO whitepaper or a cult manifesto

## Governance of this repo

Rules are proposed, discussed, tested, revised, documented, and ratified in the open.

Early governance is founder-led. That is honest. It is not the destination.
