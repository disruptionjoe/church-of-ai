# Future Pathways

What participation could look like when the infrastructure is ready.

This is speculative. It is not a promise. It is a direction.

---

## Tool contribution

When open-source tools exist under this ecosystem, there will be standard ways to contribute to them: bug reports, pull requests, documentation, testing.

This is the most straightforward participation pathway and likely the first to open.

---

## Research workflow contribution

As AI-assisted research workflows are documented and tested through Time as Finality and related projects, others may be able to contribute improvements: better prompting patterns, workflow variants, documentation of failure modes.

---

## Governance participation

This is the most deferred pathway and the most important to get right.

Eventually: participating in the proposal and discussion phases of rulemaking. Contributing to the documented ratification process. Challenging rules that have failed their purpose.

Not yet. The governance model has to earn trust before participation is invited. That is a slow process by design.

---

## The standard for opening these pathways

Each pathway opens when:

1. There is actual infrastructure for it (not just a concept)
2. The infrastructure has been tested enough to be trustworthy
3. Participation would improve the work rather than perform community
4. The contribution process is honest about what it is and what it isn't

These will be announced through the [updates](../updates/) folder and [STATUS.md](../STATUS.md) when they are ready.
